Fettucine Alfredo Recipe

Fettucine Alfredo is an Italian pasta dish of fresh fettuccine tossed with butter and Parmesan cheese. As the cheese melts, it emulsifies the liquids to form a smooth and rich cheese sauce coating the pasta. The dish is named after Alfredo di Lelio, who featured the dish at his restaurant in Rome in the early to mid-20th century; the "ceremony" of preparing it tableside was an integral part of the dish.

Ingredients

  • butter
  • minced garlic
  • whipping cream
  • Parmesan Cheese (grated)
  • garlic pepper and salt

Steps

  1. The sauce is made by melting 2 tablespoons unsalted butter and then adding the minced garlic to a large skillet on LOW-MEDIUM heat.
  2. Let this cook for about a minute and then whisk in the whipping cream.
  3. SIMMER. Bring this to a simmer and then add the Parmesan cheese. The sauce will thicken up.
  4. NOODLES. While you are making the sauce, cook up the fettuccine noodles according to package directions in a large pot.
  5. After boiling, drain water and add to sauce. Season with garlic salt and pepper.

You can also mixed in cooked veggies (broccoli is our fav) or top with cooked chicken or shrimp if desired. Garnish or sprinkle with fresh herbs (fresh Parsley, Thyme, etc) to make the dish look even more delicious.
